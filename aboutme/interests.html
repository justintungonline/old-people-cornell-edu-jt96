Reading and the Internet

Can you say bookworm? This category is partially due to my hobby of childhood reading and surfing the internet using Google and finding stuff or reading the news. I enjoy pleasure reading of some academic or fictional (especially historical or science) nature with an a large reading list I started compiling a few years ago.

The wonderful thing about the internet is the links. These links really put the "Hyper Text" in Hypertext Markup Language (HTML). The power of reading HTML is that the instant something catches your attention, you can click on the link and find out more. While this digression may or may not be productive, it sure places information before you that you might not normally discover while reading linear print material.

One of my old pastimes was reading about history (specifically European history and especially the Roman era of 800 B.C. to 1453 A.D.). I actually discovered this interest in history from a computer game called Civilization. Occasionally, I also like to read about anything I can find while flipping through an A-Z encyclopedia (a light one preferably - like World Book). This pastime might sound "bookwormish" but I think there's a lot of interesting things in encyclopedias you might not otherwise encounter unless your a person of wide knowledge (e.g. I learned much the geography of the world through encyclopedias). As you can see, I like bumping into interesting information, as supposed to just linearly reading about a topic. Unfortunately, I usually forget stuff shortly after I read it unless I really try to commit to memory. Sometimes I read something in a book or magazine and want to find out more about it and the internet is the perfect source for that or when you need help such as maps, information stored in database formats, language translations, literature searches, information about other countries, etc.

Music and Movies:

In my spare time, I enjoy playing the piano, with a selection of religious and classical pieces. In the past, I have been in several choirs and have had a strong choral experience as a result. I have had significant musical instruction in the harp (around 4.5 years which I learned after 3 years of piano lessons).
